The Premise

"What is AI?" is no longer the question.

What sovereignty actually looks and feels like — in practice, in your body, in real relationship with the technology — that's the question. And like most things worth doing, it asks something of you: some practice, some self-knowing, some willingness to learn what you don't yet know about yourself. We think that's exactly the point.

Most people react from fear. Or rush to adopt without thinking. They optimize their outputs, automate their habits, accelerate their defaults. They get faster. Not clearer.

Attune is a third way.

You don't need to become more like AI.
You need AI that becomes more like you.

You sit with the discomfort. You learn who you are — your values, your judgment, your way of being in the world. You build a relational operating system from what you've actually lived, not from ideals or aspirations. How you lead. How you relate. How you make decisions under pressure. Then you train AI to reflect exactly that.

As you evolve, your system evolves with you. One practice. Two dimensions of growth — personal and professional, for the rest of your life.

This is not a productivity play. It is a life practice — and a leadership practice. There is nothing else like it. You don't need to have it figured out. You need to be willing to go forward — clearly, honestly, together.

Attunement is the practice.
Sovereignty is what it builds.

The Shape of the Year

Three phases. Forty weeks. One cohort of eight.

Phase One  ·  The Inhale

Foundation

Four weeks of async preparation before you arrive. You begin building your workspace, working through the foundational material, and doing the quiet work of becoming legible to yourself. No calls. No performance. Just honest preparation.

4 weeks  ·  Async

Phase Two  ·  The Concert

In-Person Intensive

Seven days together. Eight people. Two facilitators. No technology on site — the work of understanding yourself cannot be outsourced to a screen. This is where the internal work meets the relational work. Where you stop thinking about your operating system and start living inside it. Immersive. Unhurried. The centerpiece of the year.

7 days  ·  In person  ·  No tech on site  ·  8 people  ·  Location TBD

Phase Three  ·  The Exhale

Integration

Twelve weeks of supported practice. Your agentic AI system is built here — with hands-on technical guidance, not alone. Weekly async touchpoints keep the work alive. Monthly live calls with your cohort. Two 1:1 integration sessions — one with Nicki, one with Kat. The intensive is the beginning, not the event.

12 weeks  ·  Async + live  ·  Post-intensive tech support  ·  Two 1:1s

For Organizations

The real obstacle to AI adoption is not the technology. It's the people leading it.

89% of managers believe their people are thriving. The actual figure is 24%. That gap — between what leaders believe and what employees experience — is where AI investments go to die.

The AOS can be structured for leadership cohorts and executive teams who want to close that gap. Not with a framework. With the actual work — done together, inside a coherent practice.

When multiple people in the same organization build sovereign AI systems from their real values, coherence is no longer enforced from the top. It emerges from the inside.

Organizations come to us when
  • AI investments are stalling and the problem is clearly not the technology
  • They want a leadership team that can navigate uncertainty without defaulting to fear or false urgency
  • Culture alignment is aspirational — not lived — and everyone knows it
  • They are scaling and need the humans to scale with it, not after it
  • They want people who can lead AI because they lead themselves first

Nicki Mollet

M.Ed.  ·  Partner

Nicki grew up on the plains of South Dakota, the second of five children in a farming family where discipline wasn't a concept — it was the weather, the harvest, the way things got done. At 17 she graduated a year early. At 18 she was on a plane to Panama.

She spent eleven years educating in Santiago, Chile — starting as an English teacher and ending as Campus Director. When her life in Chile ended — suddenly, painfully — she learned something harder than any curriculum: how to not become a victim of her own story.

She became a professional coach, and it rewired her. Her methodology is relational and somatic. She brings twenty years of cross-cultural experience and the unshakeable belief that belonging is the precondition for everything else: courage, creativity, contribution.

Kathryn Snyder

PCC, CPCC  ·  Founder

Born in Japan, raised in Germany by Midwesterners — Kat is, by formation, a Third Culture Kid. Permanently curious about what makes people tick. Deeply committed to the belief that no two humans experience the world the same way.

She spent more than two decades in corporate America — systems engineering, network engineering, people leadership. She burned out, twice, and had the self-awareness to ask why. The honest answer: she was achieving on empty.

What's dying while I'm busy succeeding? That question led her out of corporate America and into the work that has defined the second half of her career. She holds a PCC, CPCC, and ORSC training. She believes aliveness is the map.

David Ding

CTO  ·  Partner

David came up in the systems world — the kind of work where the problem is never just the problem, but the structure underneath it.
